Questions

Asked often,
answered plainly.

If your question isn't here, the intake has room for it — or it will surface naturally in your first consultation.

People at a turning of some kind — after a demanding chapter of work, a health scare survived, a decade closing — who want twelve considered weeks rather than another app and another streak to break. You don't need to be an athlete or a beginner. You need to be willing to be honest at intake.

Because questionnaires flatten people. A photograph of you, your kitchen, or the corner where you'd actually stretch in the morning tells your concierge more than twenty checkbox answers. They are context, not judgment.

You choose what to share. Guidance is given during intake, and nothing is required beyond what you're comfortable offering.

They live in a private dossier accessible only to your concierge team. They are never used in marketing, never shared, never pooled into datasets. At season's end, your dossier is returned to you or destroyed — your choice, confirmed in writing.

The season is a single flat fee of $3,800, collected securely by card at enrollment through Stripe. There are no tiers to decode and nothing sold to you mid-season. If the season doesn't proceed after your consultation — either by your judgment or ours — the fee is returned in full.

The intake takes about seven minutes. The season itself asks for a brief weekly note to your concierge and whatever time your protocol composes — which is arranged around your calendar, not on top of it. Most clients describe the season as returning time, by way of better sleep and fewer decisions.

No. Solmara is a wellness practice, not a medical provider, and your protocol never overrides the guidance of your physicians. Where your intake surfaces something that belongs in a clinician's hands, your concierge will say so plainly and adjust around it.

Each concierge carries a small number of clients so that your weekly note is read the day it arrives and your protocol is recalibrated by someone who remembers your last three. Thirty is where our attention stops stretching. When a season is full, the intake remains open for the next.

Then the season changes with it — that is the point of being accompanied. Travel, illness, a demanding month: tell your concierge and the protocol is re-composed, not abandoned. A season can also be paused once, for up to four weeks, without ceremony.
