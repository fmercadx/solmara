The Method

A season, composed
in four movements.

Everything begins quietly, with listening. Here is exactly what happens between your first answer and your final portrait.

i

A quiet beginning

Your season opens with a written intake — seven minutes of considered questions about your rhythms, your history, and what a transformed season would actually look like in your life. No forms-for-forms'-sake; every answer shapes your protocol.

Most clients complete the intake in one sitting, with something warm to drink.

ii

Your portrait

Alongside your answers, you share a small set of photographs — of yourself, your space, your table, whatever tells the truth of your current season. They give your concierge what questionnaires cannot: context.

Photographs are held in a private dossier, visible only to your concierge team, and are returned or destroyed at season's end — your choice, in writing.

iii

The composition

Within five days, your concierge composes your season: a twelve-week protocol spanning nourishment, movement, restoration, and ritual — arranged around your actual calendar, not an idealized one. You review it together in a private consultation before anything begins.

Nothing is prescribed that hasn't been discussed. You begin only when it feels composed, not assigned.

iv

Twelve weeks, accompanied

Each week closes with a short exchange between you and your concierge — what served, what didn't, what changed. Your protocol is recalibrated accordingly. By the final fortnight, the practices that proved themselves are woven into something you keep: a rhythm, not a regimen.

The season closes with a final portrait and your keepsake dossier — the record of a season composed well.

Fictional Brand — Design Concept
Solmara is a fictional business created as a portfolio design study by Francis Mercado. All names, people, testimonials, and details are invented; any resemblance to a real business is coincidental. No orders are taken and no data is collected — the onboarding flow is a working front-end demonstration only.
